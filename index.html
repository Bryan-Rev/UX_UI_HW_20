Tampa Bay
Beagle Rescue

Where beagles are loved

UX/UI Case Study

A non-profit where you can adopt adorable beagles and provide them a loving home

Overview

The main objective of recreating the existing Tampa Bay Beagle Rescue website was to showcase and improve their adoption process. The purpose of the non-profit is to rescue and find homes for neglected or abused beagles within the Tampa area.

What I was responsible for

Design Process

Empathize

In this phase of the design process we wanted to understand the user and find out what builds their trust when looking for a non-profit shelter.

Define

We defined the value proposition to solve the problem that users are having. We based this on the information gathered from the users who would use the website while also analyzing the competition.

Ideate

In the ideate phase we establish the user flows and define the adoption process.

Design

In this phase the main objective was to promote and establish a user friendly adoption process by understanding the users needs when deciding on what non-profit to adopt from.

Test

Our prototype was tested on users and then iterated to finalize our design.

What are we trying to solve?

The problem that we are trying to solve is that people are able to adopt the beagles from their website. But the process of adopting is not very clear and their is only one way to do so in a poorly made call to action. Also, their page is not consistent in its organization and the navigation is not responsive.

How can we solve this?

The goal that we have set is to improve the adoption process on the non-profits website so that they can provide beagles their forever homes.

Through our redesign of the Tampa Bay Beagle Rescue website users will be able to quickly and confidently adopt a beagle from them. By providing an easy to navigate UI and displaying important information about the beagles.
